PUSH UP
DESCRIPTION:

Push-up is a strength exercise that targets the chest, shoulders, and triceps, while also engaging the core for stability.

EQUIPMENT:

WEIGHT IS REQUIRED

INSTRUCTIONS:

To perform push-ups, start by assuming a prone position on the floor with hands placed slightly wider than shoulder-width apart and feet together. Keep the body in a straight line from head to heels, engaging the abdominal and glute muscles to stabilize the core. Lower the body by bending the elbows until the chest almost touches the ground, then push upward until the arms are fully extended. Continue performing push-ups for the desired number of repetitions, maintaining good form and controlling the movement.

STRONG ABS
DESCRIPTION:

Abdominal exercises target the muscles of the core, including the rectus abdominis, obliques, and transverse abdominis, to strengthen and tone the midsection.

EQUIPMENT:

WEIGHT IS REQUIRED

INSTRUCTIONS:

To perform weighted abdominal exercises, lie flat on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor. Hold the optional weight securely against your chest or behind your head, depending on the exercise. Engage your core muscles and lift your shoulder blades off the ground, bringing your chest towards your knees. Lower back down with control and repeat for the desired number of repetitions. Make sure to maintain proper form and avoid straining your neck or back.

Squat Video
DESCRIPTION:

Squats are a compound exercise that targets the lower body, including the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes, while also engaging the core for stability. When adding weight, it's important to hold the optional weights securely at your sides or across your shoulders to maintain balance and control throughout the movement.

EQUIPMENT:

WEIGHT IS REQUIRED for weighted squats. You can use dumbbells, a barbell, or a weighted vest to add resistance.

INSTRUCTIONS:

To perform weighted squats, stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold the optional weights securely at your sides or across your shoulders. Engage your core muscles, keep your chest up, and lower your body by bending your knees and pushing your hips back as if sitting into a chair. Keep your weight in your heels and your knees tracking over your toes. Lower until your thighs are parallel to the ground or as low as comfortable, then press through your heels to return to the starting position. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, making sure to maintain proper form throughout.
