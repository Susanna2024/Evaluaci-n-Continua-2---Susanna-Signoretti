JUMPING JACK
DESCRIPTION:

Jumping jacks, also known as star jumps, are a cardiovascular exercise that involves jumping to a position with legs spread wide and hands touching overhead, then returning to a position with the feet together and the arms at the sides.

EQUIPMENT:

No equipment needed. Jumping jacks can be performed on any flat surface.

INSTRUCTIONS:

To perform a jumping jack, start with your feet together and your arms at your sides. Jump up while simultaneously spreading your legs shoulder-width apart and raising your arms above your head. Land softly on your feet while bringing your arms and legs back to the starting position. Repeat the movement continuously at a moderate to fast pace.

Running Video
DESCRIPTION:

Running is a cardiovascular exercise that involves moving rapidly on foot, typically over long distances or for a prolonged period. It is a highly effective way to improve cardiovascular fitness, burn calories, and strengthen muscles in the legs and core.

EQUIPMENT:

No equipment needed. Running can be performed outdoors on roads, trails, or tracks, or indoors on a treadmill.

INSTRUCTIONS:

To go for a run, start by choosing a suitable route or location. Begin with a brisk walk to warm up, then gradually increase your pace to a comfortable running speed. Keep your posture tall, shoulders relaxed, and arms swinging naturally. Focus on breathing rhythmically and landing lightly on your feet with each step. Aim to maintain a steady pace or vary your intensity with intervals for a more challenging workout.

DESCRIPTION:

Skipping rope, also known as jump rope, is a high-intensity cardiovascular exercise that involves jumping over a rope that is swung over the body and under the feet. It is an efficient way to improve cardiovascular health, agility, coordination, and overall fitness.

EQUIPMENT:

A skipping rope is required for this exercise. Choose a rope that is the appropriate length for your height and skill level.

INSTRUCTIONS:

To skip rope, start by holding the handles of the rope at your sides with the rope behind you. Swing the rope overhead and jump over it as it approaches your feet. Land lightly on the balls of your feet and immediately jump again as the rope passes beneath you. Keep your elbows close to your sides and your wrists relaxed. Start with a slow pace and gradually increase your speed as you become more comfortable.
